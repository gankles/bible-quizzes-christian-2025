Bible Verses About Love

Love in Scripture: The Greek word agape represents unconditional, sacrificial love—God's love for humanity. It transcends emotion, embodying commitment, compassion, and selfless devotion. Biblical love is both a commandment and a transformative force that shapes Christian character.

Pastoral Perspective

"Love is the heartbeat of the Gospel. When Jesus summarized all the law and prophets into two commandments—love God and love your neighbor—He revealed that love is not merely an emotion but the foundation of Christian living. Every verse about love invites us into deeper relationship with God and authentic community with others."

— Rev. Dr. James Mitchell, Christian Theologian

Essential Verses on Love

Explore these transformative passages that reveal the depth and power of biblical love:

"For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life."
John 3:16
The foundation of Christian faith—God's sacrificial love for humanity.
"Love is patient, love is kind. It does not envy, it does not boast, it is not proud. It does not dishonor others, it is not self-seeking, it is not easily angered, it keeps no record of wrongs."
1 Corinthians 13:4-5
Paul's definition of love—a practical guide for Christian relationships.
"A new command I give you: Love one another. As I have loved you, so you must love one another. By this everyone will know that you are my disciples, if you love one another."
John 13:34-35
Jesus' central command—love as the defining mark of discipleship.
"Dear friends, let us love one another, for love comes from God. Everyone who loves has been born of God and knows God. Whoever does not love does not know God, because God is love."
1 John 4:7-8
Love as the essence of God's nature and the evidence of spiritual rebirth.
"And now these three remain: faith, hope and love. But the greatest of these is love."
1 Corinthians 13:13
Love's supremacy among Christian virtues—the ultimate expression of faith.
"Love your enemies, do good to those who hate you, bless those who curse you, pray for those who mistreat you."
Luke 6:27-28
Jesus' radical call to extend love beyond natural boundaries.
"Let no debt remain outstanding, except the continuing debt to love one another, for whoever loves others has fulfilled the law."
Romans 13:8
Love as the fulfillment of all moral law and ethical obligation.
"Above all, love each other deeply, because love covers a multitude of sins."
1 Peter 4:8
Love's redemptive power—its ability to heal and restore relationships.
"This is how we know what love is: Jesus Christ laid down his life for us. And we ought to lay down our lives for our brothers and sisters."
1 John 3:16
Love defined by sacrifice—the ultimate expression of Christian commitment.
"Beloved, if God so loved us, we also ought to love one another. No one has ever seen God; but if we love one another, God lives in us and his love is made complete in us."
1 John 4:11-12
Love as the visible manifestation of God's presence in our lives.
"So God created mankind in his own image, in the image of God he created them; male and female he created them."
Genesis 1:27
The foundation for loving others—recognizing God's image in every person.
"Therefore, as God's chosen people, holy and dearly loved, clothe yourselves with compassion, kindness, humility, gentleness and patience."
Colossians 3:12
Love expressed through virtues—the practical clothing of Christian character.
"The greatest commandment is this: 'Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind.' And the second is like it: 'Love your neighbor as yourself.'"
Matthew 22:37-39
Jesus' summary of all Scripture—love for God and neighbor as life's purpose.
"Hatred stirs up conflict, but love covers over all wrongs."
Proverbs 10:12
Wisdom's perspective—love as the antidote to conflict and division.
"And over all these virtues put on love, which binds them all together in perfect unity."
Colossians 3:14
Love as the binding force—the unifying principle of Christian community.
