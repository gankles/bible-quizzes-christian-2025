Biblical Biography

David was the youngest son of Jesse of Bethlehem and became the second king of Israel. Born around 1040 BC, he rose from humble shepherd boy to become one of the most significant figures in biblical history. His reign lasted approximately 40 years, during which he unified the kingdom, established Jerusalem as the capital, and expanded Israel's borders. David is remembered not only as a military leader and king but also as a poet and musician, credited with composing many of the Psalms. His life was marked by both great triumphs and profound struggles, making him a complex and deeply human figure in Scripture.
